Executive summary
Across 8 major Indian cities in June 2026, air quality ranges from Good (Bangalore, AQI 44) to Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (Kolkata, AQI 103). Gurugram — your current location — sits at AQI 73 (Moderate), with PM2.5 at ~28 µg/m³ and PM10 at ~62 µg/m³, both exceeding WHO guidelines. The NCR corridor (Delhi–Gurugram–Noida) continues to be a hotspot, with Gurugram logging 156 bad-air days in 2024–25 — making it India's 2nd most polluted city by that metric. Water quality is a parallel concern: Haryana's industrial waste and domestic sewage contaminate Yamuna tributaries, placing Gurugram's water score at 38/100. Southern cities (Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad) consistently outperform northern peers on both air and water metrics.
Most surprising observation: Gurugram — despite its affluent, tech-sector profile — ranks as the 2nd most polluted city in India by bad-air days (156 days in 2024–25), worse than Delhi in this metric. Industrial corridors IMT Manesar and Udyog Vihar are key contributors alongside vehicle emissions.

Winter (Nov–Feb) — peak pollution

Peak pollution season. Wear N95 daily. Keep air purifiers running 24/7. Avoid morning outdoor exercise — wait until AQI dips after noon. Consider work-from-home on smog days (AQI 200+).

Monsoon (Jul–Sep) — best window

Best air quality of the year. Exercise outdoors, air out rooms, reduce purifier use. Watch for waterborne disease risk from contaminated rainwater runoff mixing with urban sewage.

Summer (Mar–May) — dust spikes

Construction dust and heat-driven PM10 spikes. Hydrate well, use SPF 50+ sunscreen daily, antioxidant serums. Avoid midday outdoor exposure (11 AM–4 PM).

Post-monsoon (Oct) — rapid decline

AQI can jump 50→200+ in days as stubble burning starts in Punjab/Haryana. Prepare air purifiers, stock N95 masks, seal window gaps before October 1st.

  • CriticalInstall HEPA air purifier — HEPA H13 reduces indoor PM2.5 by up to 85%. Choose CADR ≥ 300 m³/hr for a 200 sq ft room. Run continuously during winter months.
  • CriticalRO+UV water purifier — Gurugram groundwater contains nitrates, heavy metals, and coliform bacteria from Yamuna industrial discharge. Boiling alone is insufficient for chemical contaminants.
  • HighN95 mask outdoors (Oct–Feb) — pollution surges 3–5× during winter. N95-rated masks filter 95% of PM2.5. Replace every 2–3 days of heavy use.
  • HighSet up AQI alerts — CPCB Sameer app or IQAir. Set threshold at AQI 100 (moderate action) and AQI 150 (restrict outdoor activity).
  • ModerateAntioxidant skincare — PM2.5 penetrates pores and generates free radicals. AM: Vitamin C serum + SPF 50. PM: double cleanse + Niacinamide to repair barrier damage.
  • ModerateHair protection — Gurugram's hard, contaminated water accelerates thinning. Use filtered water for hair rinse, weekly clarifying shampoo, leave-in conditioner as pollution barrier.
  • SupportiveAnti-pollution diet — turmeric (curcumin), amla, green tea, omega-3s (walnuts, flaxseed) neutralize PM2.5-induced oxidative stress and support lung lining integrity.
  • SupportiveIndoor plants — snake plants, spider plants, peace lilies reduce VOCs. Complement (not replace) air purifiers in low-traffic spaces like bedrooms and offices.

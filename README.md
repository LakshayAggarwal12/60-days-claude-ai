## 👋 Hey, I'm Lakshay

I'm an engineering student with a deep interest in how technology shapes the world — and an even deeper obsession with building things that actually work.

My technical interests span **Full-Stack Development**, **Artificial Intelligence & LLMs**, **Data Structures & Problem Solving**, and the craft of writing clean, maintainable code. But beyond the tech stack, what drives me is the belief that the most effective way to learn is to build — not consume, not watch, not bookmark — but **ship**.

This challenge is my structured attempt to go from *curious about AI* to *confident with AI*, one day at a time.

---

## 🎯 What This Challenge Is

The **60 Days Claude AI Challenge** is a self-imposed commitment to explore, build, and document my journey with Claude AI and the broader AI/LLM ecosystem — every single day for 60 days.

No skipping. No passive learning. Just consistent, hands-on practice.

This repository serves as my:
- 📓 **Daily progress log** — what I worked on and what I learned
- 🔬 **Experiment notebook** — prompts, ideas, failures, and discoveries
- 🛠️ **Project showcase** — AI-powered apps and mini-projects built along the way
- 💭 **Reflection space** — honest notes on challenges and improvements

## 🚀 Goals for the Next 60 Days

### 🧠 AI & LLM Skills
- Understand how modern Large Language Models actually work
- Master Claude's capabilities, quirks, and limitations through hands-on use
- Develop strong **prompt engineering** intuition (zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought, etc.)
- Implement **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** from scratch
- Work with the **Anthropic API** and build real integrations
- Explore AI workflows, agents, and automation pipelines

### 💻 Development Skills
- Build at least **3 complete AI-powered projects**
- Improve Git discipline — meaningful commits, clean history
- Write modular, well-documented, production-quality code
- Learn best practices for structuring AI application projects
- Strengthen debugging skills specifically for LLM-based systems

### 🌱 Personal Growth
- Stay consistent for all 60 days — no zero days
- Document learnings clearly enough that others can learn from them too
- Build a portfolio that reflects genuine skill, not tutorial-following
- Develop stronger instincts for problem decomposition and system design

## 📌 Challenge Rules (My Own)

1. **No zero days** — even 20 minutes of focused work counts
2. **Build over browse** — always favor building something over reading about it
3. **Document honestly** — log failures and confusion, not just wins
4. **Ship something every week** — at least one tangible output per 7 days
5. **Reflect weekly** — one review post per week to consolidate learning

📖Nutrition Literacy
🔬 Why Micronutrients Matter
Micronutrients like Iron, Calcium, and Vitamins are essential cofactors for metabolic reactions. Deficiency — even subclinical — can impair cognition, immune function, and energy metabolism over weeks to months.
⚡ Understanding TDEE
Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is calculated using Mifflin-St Jeor BMR × Physical Activity Level (PAL). It estimates your maintenance calories — the baseline around which deficits or surpluses are applied.
🫀 Saturated Fat & Sodium
Chronic high saturated fat intake is linked to elevated LDL cholesterol. Excess sodium intake (>2300mg/day) is associated with hypertension. NutriScope tracks fat totals; subcategories require lab-level data.
🌿 Dietary Fibre & Gut Health
Soluble fibre feeds beneficial gut bacteria and slows glucose absorption. Insoluble fibre promotes bowel regularity. The Indian RDA is 30g/day; most urban diets fall 30–50% short of this target.

Data Sources
Scientific references and databases used by NutriScope
📚
All nutrient data is sourced from authoritative public databases. Values are per 100g of edible portion unless specified. Data accuracy depends on preparation method, food variety, and regional differences.
🇮🇳 ICMR–NIN (2020)
Indian Council of Medical Research – National Institute of Nutrition. "Nutrient Requirements for Indians." Primary source for RDA values including protein, micronutrients, and energy targets for the Indian population.
nin.res.in · icmr.gov.in
🌐 USDA FoodData Central
United States Department of Agriculture comprehensive food composition database. Used for macronutrient and selected micronutrient values for foods not fully covered by Indian databases.
fdc.nal.usda.gov
🌍 WHO Nutrient Requirements
World Health Organization joint FAO/WHO expert consultation reports on human vitamin and mineral requirements. Used for Vitamin D, B12, and trace mineral RDAs.
who.int/nutrition
📗 Nutritive Value of Indian Foods (NVIF)
Published by NIN, this reference provides composition data for >1000 Indian foods. NutriScope's Indian food values (Idli, Poha, Dosa, Dal varieties, etc.) are derived from this publication.
nin.res.in/downloads/DietaryGuidelinesforNINwebsite.pdf
📐 Mifflin-St Jeor Equation
Mifflin MD, et al. (1990). "A new predictive equation for resting energy expenditure in healthy individuals." Am J Clin Nutr 51(2):241–7. Used for BMR calculation; validated as most accurate for diverse populations.
doi:10.1093/ajcn/51.2.241
🧬 FAO/WHO/UNU (2004)
Human Energy Requirements: Report of a Joint FAO/WHO/UNU Expert Consultation. Used for Physical Activity Level (PAL) multipliers: Sedentary 1.2 → Extra Active 1.9.
fao.org/3/y5686e/y5686e.pdf
⚠️ Limitations & Disclaimer
Nutrient values are estimates and vary by cooking method, variety, soil quality, and storage. This tool does not account for bioavailability differences, drug-nutrient interactions, or medical conditions. NutriScope is for educational use only.
